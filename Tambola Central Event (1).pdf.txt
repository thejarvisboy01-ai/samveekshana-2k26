The Basics 
The Ticket: Each ticket has a 3x9 grid with 15 numbers (5 per row).  
The Caller: One person is designated as the "Caller" to randomly draw numbers 
from 1 to 90 and announce them.  
Marking: When the caller announces a number that is on your ticket, you strike it 
off.  
 
Common Winning Patterns 
The game typically pauses for each "win" to be verified by the caller.   
Early Five: The first person to strike off any five numbers on their ticket.  
Top Line: Completing all 5 numbers in the top horizontal row.  
Middle Line: Completing all 5 numbers in the middle horizontal row.  
Bottom Line: Completing all 5 numbers in the bottom horizontal row.  
Four Corners: Marking the first and last numbers of both the top and bottom rows. 
Full House: Striking off all 15 numbers on the tick et. This is the grand prize.  
 
Rules of Play 
Claims: You must claim your win immediately after the number is called. If the 
caller has already drawn the next number, the claim might be considered invalid 
in some variations. 
Verification: The caller must ve rify the ticket by checking the struck numbers 
against the board of called numbers.  
Tie-Breaker: If multiple players claim the same winning pattern simultaneously, 
the prize is usually split equally between them
